To describe bottom trawling makes you sound insane. Dragging literal tons of steel across the seabed, tearing up clams, corals, cockles and every other c under the sea. The destruction wreaked upon the earth would be intolerable on land. The practice is only possible because this terrible violence takes place beneath the obscuring lens of the ocean. On the surface, the fishing captain pulls a lever, or more often a slider on a dashboard, the vessel moves forward with a little more purpose atop an azure calm sea. Far beneath the waves, land is laid to waste, entire ecosystems destroeyed, all for a scant few fish. fewer each year as the catch per unit effort dwindles in our overfished ocean.

Why do we persist with this? Mores even than tolerating, many of us subsidise this destruction through tax breaks and direct suppoort to "nationally critical" fishing fleets that make up less than a rounding error of our economy and a miniscule proportion of the workforce. Why do they have such a strnglehold on our collective imagination?
