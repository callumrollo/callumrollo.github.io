About

I am currently a final year PhD student at the University of East Anglia (UEA) in Norwich, UK. I work with marine gliders, specifically the integration on Acoustic Doppler Current Profilers (ADCPs) onto gliders to directly measure currents in the ocean. The specific glider I work with is SG637 "Omura" equipped with a 1MHz ADCP.

Data and live location information from this glider, and others in the fleet, is available on the UEA glider website. I am open to collaborations in physical oceanography. Especially projects involving ADCPs on autonomous platforms, shelf break processes and open source science initiatives.

I am an amateur Pythonista, leader of UEA python and proponent of free software for science. Several of my projects are available on my github.
